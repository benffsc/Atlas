Step 1: Find

A community member reports cats on their street

Every request starts with a person reaching out. We capture the location, the situation, and how to help — then find it on the map.

Step 2: Fix

We bring them to our clinic

FFSC is the only dedicated spay/neuter clinic for community cats in Sonoma County. Every cat gets a medical record, microchip, and ear tip.

Step 3: Return

They go home — and we track the whole neighborhood

Each site isn’t isolated. Beacon connects every colony to the places around it, building a regional picture of progress.

Step 4: Analyze

Beacon shows us where to focus next

Population estimates, alteration rates, seasonal trends — data-driven decisions for every zone in the county.
